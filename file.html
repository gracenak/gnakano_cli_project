Tucson Brewery Guide: Where you can stay & sip or grab it to-go

Matt SternerMay 21, 20200 Comment
Photo courtesy of Pueblo Vida Brewing Company on Facebook

You've got options when it comes to enjoying a refreshing & local beer in Tucson.

Local breweries have experienced their share of slowdowns while keeping their employees safe during the current pandemic.Some have temporarily closed their doors, while others have resorted to to-go orders only.

As of late, certain breweries have chosen to open their seating areas at limited capacity, giving the option to stay and grab a beer, with the option of taking some home, too.

Here’s a list of who’s fully open, who isn’t, and who is offering delivery and to-go beers.

1912 Brewing Company

2045 Forbes Blvd. Ste. 105

View this post on Instagram

We are open today 3-8pm! ? Stop by for some dinner… @danielascooking special will be Chorizo Con Papas tacos, a perfect pair with the Irish Sobriety Red Ale! ?

A post shared by 1912 Brewing Company (@1912brewing) on May 19, 2020 at 11:41am PDT

1912 Brewing Company is currently open with limited hours and you’re welcome to have a drink inside their taproom six feet apart, of course. Daniela’s Cookingis often posted out in front, too, giving you another way to support local.

For more information, visit 1912brewing.com.

The Address Brewing

1702 E. Speedway Blvd.

View this post on Instagram

Pizza, wings, and beer (cans, bottles, growler fills) available Monday through Saturday, 4:30 to 8:30! Thank you for your help keeping everyone safe, Tucson. And thank you for continuing to support us while doing so!

A post shared by 1702 (@1702.theaddress) on May 12, 2020 at 6:54pm PDT

The Address Brewing’s dining room is currently closed, but that doesn’t mean you can’t order a growler of beer and some pizza for home.

Their temporary hours are 4:30 – 8:30 p.m. every Monday – Saturday. You can order online or by calling (520) 325-1702.

For more information, visit1702az.com

Barrio Brewing Company

800 E. 16th St.

View this post on Instagram

Finish your weekend off the right way… with a beer! ? Were open till 8 for dine-in and pick-up!

A post shared by Barrio Brewing Co. (@barriobrewing) on May 17, 2020 at 4:06pm PDT

The taproom is open at Barrio Brewing Companyand you’ve still got the option to order everything to-go as well.

Sunday – Thursday they’re open from 11 a.m. – 8 p.m. and from 11 a.m. – 9 p.m. on Friday and Saturday. If you’d rather take your beer and food home, you can order online or call (520) 791-2739.

For more information, visit barriobrewing.com.

BlackRock Brewers

1664 S. Research Loop, Ste 200

View this post on Instagram

Weve spent the day measuring, arranging, and taping off 6ft boundaries for opening on Tuesday. Courtneys behind the bar with her bleach ready to clean and pour you a growler. #beer #craftbeer #azbeer #tucson #beerstagram #beerme #saturdayvibes #beerlover #drinklocal #supportlocal

A post shared by BlackRock Brewers (@blackrock_brewers) on May 9, 2020 at 4:54pm PDT

BlackRock Brewers opened up its taproom again with limited seating, and have been cleaning and taping off boundaries for the safety of six feet. They’re also selling some new Lunch Box Sandwichesfor anyone craving a bite to eat there or at home.

Their hours of operation may still be limited, so make sure to check on that beforehand.

For more information, visitblackrockbrewers.com.

Borderlands Brewing Company

119 E. Toole Ave.

View this post on Instagram

We know its going to be a hot one today ?? So come and get any refreshing beer TO-GO from 12 – 8pm or get it delivered right to your doorstep ? Reminder, were open every Thursday through Sunday from 12 – 8pm #cheers

A post shared by Borderlands Brewing Co. (@borderlandsbrewing) on May 17, 2020 at 12:10pm PDT

If you’re looking for a nice, cold beer on a hot and sunny day, hit up Borderland Brewing Company because they’re offering to-go and delivery brews (within a five-mile radius of the taproom).

They’re open from noon – 8 p.m. on Thursday – Sunday, but you can order any day of the week with their new online store.

For more information, visit borderlandsbrewing.com.

Button Brew House

6800 N. Camino Martin, Ste 160

View this post on Instagram

Thursday 5/21 Deliveries ALL of Tucson Friday 5/22 Deliveries in Phoenix Order at bit.ly/bbh-online or 520.268.8543 or drink@buttonbrew.com #supportsmallbusiness #chooseazbrews

A post shared by Button Brew House (@buttonbrewhouse) on May 20, 2020 at 9:22am PDT

The northwest side brewery,Button Brew House, is delivering beer as far as the east side of Tucson. The taproom remains closed but you can pick up your to-go order or request delivery. Their hours vary, too, depending on the day, but you can order online or by calling (520) 268-8543.

Keep in mind, the brewery is temporarily closed on Tuesdays.

For more information, visit buttonbrewhouse.com.

Catalina Brewing Company

6918 N. Camino Martin, Ste. 120

View this post on Instagram

Taproom and patio are open, El Classico IPA is back on tap, and weve got trivia and pizza tomorrow night!

A post shared by Catalina Brewing Company (@catalinabrewingco) on May 14, 2020 at 7:15pm PDT

Located around the corner from Button Brew House, Catalina Brewing Company has reopened their taproom and patio for those who’d like to stick around, along with the option to get your beer to-go.

Catalina Brewing’s hours of operation appear to be back to the regular schedule, with a lineup of recurring food trucks and happy hour pricing.

For more information, visit catalinabrewingco.com.

Copper Mine Brewing Company

3455 S. Palo Verde Rd.

View this post on Instagram

@molecular.munchies everyday, weekend specials, and hours for May!! . . . . #copperminebrew #weeklyschedule #eventswillbebacksoon #molecularmunchies #chooseazbrews #azretapped #beerandburgers #girlsbehindthebar

A post shared by Copper Mine Brewing (@copperminebrew) on May 19, 2020 at 2:37pm PDT

Copper Mine Brewing Company is allowing up to 20 people at a time in their taproom. They’re planning to be open from 4 – 9 p.m. on Monday – Friday, 2 – 9 p.m. on Saturday, and noon – 6 p.m. on Sunday.

In addition, you can still get your growler filled with your favorite brew and enjoy in the comfort of your home.

Keep up withCopper Mine Brewing Company on Facebook.

Keep up with Corbett Brewing Company on Facebook.

Crooked Tooth Brewing Company’s taproom is closed but you can still grab any of their brews on the go or by delivery. Also, they’ve been busy with minor renovations and installations, including the brand new foeder they managed to squeeze in.

Currently, their hours of operation are 3 – 8 p.m. on Monday – Friday, noon – 6 p.m. on Saturday and Sunday, and are delivering within a five-mile radius from noon – 4 p.m. on Tuesday – Friday.

These are the temporary hours so follow their social media accounts for any updates.

Keep up with Crooked Tooth on Facebook.

View this post on Instagram

Dillinger Brewing Companyis making the best of their time during the pandemic, and have been sharing some pretty entertaining videos to lure in thirsty patrons.

For more information, visitdillingerbrewing.com.

View this post on Instagram

Crowler Hour 5-6pm DM your orders ??

A post shared by Public Brewhouse (@publicbrewhouse) on Apr 29, 2020 at 4:54pm PDT

Public Brewhouse is keeping the taproom closed but has created a new “Crowler Hour” from 5 – 6 p.m. daily. The brewery has been keeping their tap list updated on its social media accounts.

Here’s how it works:
  • DM your choice of beer on either Instagram or Facebook
  • Pick it up between 5 – 6 p.m.
  • Sit back, relax, and sip away once your home or anywhere else besides the taproom

Ten55 Brewing Company

110 E. Congress St.

View this post on Instagram

To our friends, neighbors and guests, The restaurant is eerily quiet without you, we miss you! We thought it would be a good idea to let everyone know what's happening here at Ten 55. We've had many long conversations about what the next few weeks looks like for us and the rest of the Ten 55 team. The decision of when to reopen is an intensely difficult one, but after much consideration, we've concluded that it just doesn't make sense for us to reopen our dining room quite yet. There's just no higher priority for us than the safety of our staff and their families. Furthermore, the economics of our situation reinforce the need for us to wait, as a downtown food and beverage venue, our traffic is closely related to all the fun stuff that happens downtown, and sadly none of those things are happening quite yet. We're super appreciative of the love and support you've all shown coming down to get takeout beer, and we're excited to announce that we're moving towards bringing our kitchen back online for takeout food as well! We're just starting that process, but in a couple weeks we look forward to having our takeout menu up and running! The next few months hold some exciting things for Ten 55, including a sour bottle release in collaboration with our homies over at Whiskey Del Bac, new packaging options, new apparel and merch, and maybe a couple top secret projects too…… You're the reason we do this, and it makes our hearts heavy that we won't see you for a few more weeks, but we'll get thru this before we know it! Thanks for your continued love and support, we'll have a beer together soon. (Photo By @yelptucson Elite: Paige A.)

Plus, they’ve incorporated an online ordering system, which appears to be operational. Be sure to follow their social media accounts for any other updates.

For more information, visit 1055brewing.com.
